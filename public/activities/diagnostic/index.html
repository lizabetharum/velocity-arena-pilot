Reference Card
Speed and distance
Speed tells you how far something travels in a given amount of time.
Distance = Speed × Time
A runner at 8 m/s for 10 seconds covers 80 meters. If speed changes, add up each section separately.
Graphs
A graph shows a relationship between two quantities. The x-axis usually shows input (often time). The y-axis shows output (distance, height, cost).

A straight line = constant relationship. Steeper = faster change. Flat = no change.
Slope
Slope tells you how much y changes for every 1 unit increase in x.
Slope = (change in y) ÷ (change in x)
Slope of 3: every time x goes up 1, y goes up 3. Negative slope = y is decreasing.
Ordered pairs
An ordered pair (x, y) gives a location on a coordinate grid. x = left/right. y = up/down.

Proportional reasoning
Two quantities are proportional if their ratio stays the same.
2 items = $6 → 5 items = $15 (always $3 per item)
To check: divide. If the answer is always the same, it is proportional.
Rate of change
How fast something is increasing or decreasing.
Rate = (change in output) ÷ (change in input)
Battery drops from 80% to 20% in 4 hours: rate = −15% per hour.
